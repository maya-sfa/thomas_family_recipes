1 lbs beef chuck, cubed
24 oz Andouille Sausage (2 packs), removed from casing
16 oz Red Kidney Beans
1 medium onion, chopped
2 bell peppers, chopped
4 stalks celery, chopped
2 Thai chilis, chopped, with seeds
12 cloves garlic, minced
1 1/2 tsp salt
1 Tbsp cocoa powder
2 Tbsp Cajun seasoning
1 tsp smoked paprika
1 bay leaf
1/4 C tomato paste
4 C beef stock
12 oz lager or white ale
2 Tbsp bacon fat or butter

This recipe can be made in a slow cooker, or in a large pot on the
stove. If making in the slow cooker, the initial steps should be
performed in a large skillet.

Brown the sausage in the large skillet or pot, taking care to break
the sausage up as it browns. If cooking in a slow cooker, remove the
sausage from skillet with a slotted spoon, and add it to the slow
cooker.

Add the bacon fat or butter to the skillet or pot over medium
heat. Once it is melted, add the onion, pepper, celery, and Thai
chilis, and sweat for 7 - 10 minutes or until the onion is
translucent.

Add the garlic and heat until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

If cooking in a slow cooker, place this mixture into the slow cooker.

Add the stock and beer to the pot or slow cooker, then the beef, salt,
cocoa powder, Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, bay leaf, and tomato
paste.

Stil to combine. If cooking on the slow cooker, set to cook for eight
hours on low. If cooking on the stove, raise to a boil, then reduce
heat to a simmer and cover. Cook eight hours, stirring occassionally.
